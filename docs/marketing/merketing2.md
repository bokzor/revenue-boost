The "Revenue Boost" app is essentially an "All-in-One" Conversion Rate Optimization (CRO) tool. This places you in direct competition with giants like Vitals and Sales Hunter, as well as niche leaders like ReConvert (upsells) and Privy (popups).

To improve your listing, you must solve the "Swiss Army Knife" problem: merchants often search for a specific tool (e.g., "spin wheel" or "upsell cart"), not a generic "revenue booster." If you try to be everything, you risk ranking for nothing.

The following advice focuses on Shopify App Store Optimization (ASO) and Conversion Rate Optimization (CRO) for your listing itself.
1. The "Hook": Title & Subtitle Optimization

Your current name, "Revenue Boost," is descriptive but generic. In 2025, the Shopify algorithm heavily weights keywords in the Title and Subtitle.

    Current Issue: "Revenue Boost" doesn't tell the algorithm what the app is (Popup? Upsell? Bundle?).

    Strategy: Adopt a "Brand Name + Primary Keywords" structure. You need to anchor your app to the highest-volume search terms: Upsell, Bundles, Popups, and Cross Sell.

Recommended Title Formulas (Max 30 characters is ideal for branding, but use up to the limit for SEO if allowed/readable):

    Option A (Upsell Focus): Revenue Boost: Upsell & Bundles

    Option B (All-in-One Focus): Revenue Boost: Popups & Upsell

    Option C (Growth Focus): Revenue Boost: CRO & Sales Pop

Recommended Subtitle (Max 62 characters):

    Current implied: Grow email list, increase revenue, and engage customers. (Too vague).

    Better: Upsell bundles, Email Popups, Cross sell, & 20+ Sales Tools.

        Why: It hits 4 major keywords immediately.

2. The "Key Benefits" Section (The Make or Break)

Shopify displays 3 "Key Benefit" blocks prominently. Do not use these to list generic features like "Analytics." Use them to solve specific pain points.

    Benefit 1: The Revenue Driver (Upsells)

        Header: Increase AOV with Smart Upsells

        Body: One-click upsells, frequent bought together bundles, and in-cart cross-sells that feel native, not spammy.

    Benefit 2: The Lead Generator (Gamification)

        Header: Capture 3x More Emails

        Body: Convert visitors with interactive Spin-to-Win wheels, exit-intent popups, and teaser styling that stops abandonment.

    Benefit 3: The Closer (Social Proof & Urgency)

        Header: Build Trust & Urgency

        Body: Automate social proof notifications, stock countdowns, and free shipping bars to nudge hesitant buyers.

3. Visuals: Screenshots & Video

Your feature list is huge (16 templates!). If you just show screenshots of settings panels, you will lose users.

    The "3-Second Rule": The first screenshot must show the result, not the UI.

        Slide 1: A split screen showing a "Sad Cart" ($20) vs. a "Revenue Boost Cart" ($45 with Upsell + Free Gift). Text overlay: "Boost AOV Instantly."

    Show, Don't Tell:

        Don't show the "Spin to Win" settings page. Show the actual Wheel popup on a mobile screen.

        Don't show the "Targeting" dashboard. Show a graphic: "Target by: Device, Cart Value, & Location."

    Video: Essential for "All-in-One" apps. You need a 30-60 second video that cycles rapidly through the tools: “Need a Popup? We got it. Need Upsells? Done. Need Bundles? Easy.”

4. Description: Restructuring for "Scannability"

Merchants scan; they don't read. Your current list is comprehensive but dense. Structure it by Goal, not by Feature Type.

Proposed Structure:

    Stop paying for 10 different apps. Revenue Boost gives you the complete conversion stack—Upsells, Popups, Bundles, and Gamification—in one lightweight app. Faster load times, unified data, and a single monthly bill.

Focus on the money-making features.

    Smart Upsells & Cross-Sells: Recommend matching products right in the cart drawer.

    Bundle & Save: Amazon-style "Frequently Bought Together" widgets.

    Goal-Based Progress Bars: "Spend $15 more for Free Shipping."

Focus on the lead gen features.

    Gamified Popups: Spin-to-Win and Scratch Cards (proven to convert 3x better than standard forms).

    Exit Intent Technology: Catch visitors right before they leave.

    Two-Step Opt-ins: Non-intrusive "teasers" that expand on click.

Focus on the psychological triggers.

    Social Proof: "Someone in New York just bought..." notifications.

    Scarcity Timers: Countdown timers for flash sales.

    Create a simple text table showing: "Competitor A ($29) + Competitor B ($19) + Competitor C ($9) = $57/mo" vs "Revenue Boost = $9/mo".

5. Pricing Strategy Advice

Your pricing ($9 / $29 / $79) is aggressive, which is good for acquisition, but be careful of the "Cheap = Low Quality" perception.

    The $9 "Starter" Plan: Ensure this includes enough value to be sticky (e.g., allow 1 Upsell + 1 Popup), but restrict the "High ROI" features (like A/B Testing) to the $29 plan.

    The "No-Brainer" Anchor: Positioning the $29 plan as "The Growth Suite" is smart. Explicitly state in the description that this plan "Replaces apps costing $150+/mo."

    Free Plan? You didn't mention a Free tier. In this crowded market, a "Free Forever" plan (limited by views, e.g., 1,000 views) is essentially required to get initial traction and reviews. If you don't have one, consider it.

6. Technical Differentiation

Since you mentioned features like "Theme App Extension" and "Zero Configuration," highlight these!

    "Won't Slow Down Your Site": This is a huge fear for merchants installing "All-in-One" apps. Explicitly state that you use Shopify Theme App Extensions to ensure zero code bloat.

    "Mobile-First Design": Mention that your "Minimal Slide-Up" and "Bottom Sheet" templates are specifically designed to pass Google's "Intrusive Interstitial" penalties and work perfectly on mobile.
